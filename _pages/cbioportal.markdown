<p class="detail-lead">
cBioPortal for Cancer Genomics is one of the most widely used platforms for visualizing and analyzing cancer genomics data. The platform is fully open source and actively maintained by KSG @ Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, <a href="https://www.mskcc.org/">Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center</a>, <a href="https://www.princessmargaret.ca/">Princess Margaret Cancer Centre</a>, and <a href="http://thehyve.nl">The Hyve</a>. KSG also maintains a local instance — cBioPortal @ DFCI — enabling researchers to explore all genomic data generated by the Profile project.
</p>

## Key Capabilities

<div class="feature-grid">
  <div class="feature-card">
    <div class="feature-card-title">OncoPrint</div>
    <p class="feature-card-desc">Visual summary of genomic alterations across a cohort of patients — mutations, copy number changes, and fusions in a single view.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-card">
    <div class="feature-card-title">Mutation Analysis</div>
    <p class="feature-card-desc">Explore somatic mutations across genes and cancer types, with lollipop plots and protein domain annotations.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-card">
    <div class="feature-card-title">Survival Analysis</div>
    <p class="feature-card-desc">Kaplan-Meier survival curves stratified by genomic alterations, expression levels, or clinical attributes.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-card">
    <div class="feature-card-title">Gene Expression</div>
    <p class="feature-card-desc">Compare mRNA expression across cancer types, patient cohorts, and genomic subgroups.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-card">
    <div class="feature-card-title">Multi-Study Analysis</div>
    <p class="feature-card-desc">Query and compare data across hundreds of published cancer studies simultaneously.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-card">
    <div class="feature-card-title">Clinical Integration</div>
    <p class="feature-card-desc">Correlate genomic findings with clinical data including tumor type, treatment history, and outcomes.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## cBioPortal @ DFCI

In addition to the public platform, KSG maintains a local instance of cBioPortal at Dana-Farber. This private instance gives DFCI researchers secure access to genomic data from the Profile project — one of the most comprehensive clinical sequencing efforts in the US — enabling them to explore patient-level data and drive research discoveries directly from clinical sequencing results.
